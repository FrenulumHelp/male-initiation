I was also concerned about the teeth pulling. I am hoping that these were not permanent teeth. In the United States there is a major concern to keep even baby teeth healthy, and dentists will regularly fill cavities in baby teeth. I originally thought this was another "money maker" for the dentists and I still think there is some truth to this; however, my dentist explained that if the baby teeth are not filled, then it has proven that the decay spreads to upward to the perfectly healthy adult tooth that is forming...and that orthodonitia is easier if the teeth remain in and the new teeth come in when they are ready. Pulling baby teeth prematurely can cause unnecessary orthodontia work. I thought both of these explanations sounded reasonable. Many children in the U.S. start with an orthodontist at age 7,8,9, and up because the orthodontis can help the teeth come in correctly, which reduces the time and need for long term braces and wires. To tell you the difference in medicine, when I was 14, the orthodontist at the time had four of my regular teeth, plus all of my wisdom teeth pulled because I had a "small" mouth...Great now I had to get braces again because my teeth started moving inwards to where it was really hurting my mouth. The current orthodontist: "we don't pull teeth now unless absolutely necessary...you will have to have braces again, and wear a retainer at night for life to prevent the slippage" SO please pass this type of message on to the grandparents of your 8 year old...and let them know that they will not take his health in their hands...that you are the parent and you will decide upon consultation directly with the doctor. If he is visiting them, fine, but you will still speak to any doctor before any procedure happens to him
Otherwise, I would not send him there for fear of what else could happen to him when you are not there to intervene.
Hope this helps

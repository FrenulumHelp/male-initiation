Certainly you don't have paraphimosis because that is a condition in which a very tight ring of scar tissue in the foreskin cuts off circulation to the glans when the ring is pushed back behind the glans, and in serious cases can threaten the glans with gangrene. Your condition on the other hand is perfectly normal, and you share it with thousands of other men and boys. It just results from having a foreskin that is naturally short, so it doesn't cover the entire glans. There is certainly no cause for worry on your part, and every reason just to relax and enjoy it (which your girlfriend may do, too)! Some doctors have called it "autocircumcision" and the "circumcisionoid" condition, and when I was in the army the guys called it being "skinned back," but I'm not sure it deserves a name: it's just at one end of the normal bell curve encompassing natural variation, where most guys have just enough skin to cover the glans and others (at the other end of the curve) have extra skin that overhangs the end of the penis. End of lecture! Seriously, you're lucky and have no worries. Cheers, RJK.

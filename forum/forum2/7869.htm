Read down a bit. Try this post for starters. The exercises illustrated there utilise pulling forward simply because it is easy and comfortable to do. While they may seem to be tending to elongate the foreskin, that would be a very minor effect. Skin stretching is a "non-linear" process - small amounts of tension cause virtually no stretch unless applied nearly 100% of the time, while more and more tension produces stretching, albeit slow, but in disproportionate amounts.

Painful stretching can produce very rapid results indeed, but apart form the pain, is difficult to control and may result in some tearing and scarring, which can be counterproductive (unless the stretching can and will be repeated regularly until healing is complete). For that reason, we always advise stretching that is not painful - just a little uncomfortable.

The point being, that only the part you stretch tight will respond. This means, that pulling the lower foreskin forward is useful (probably the most useful way) to stretch the fraenulum as it being the short part, takes the brunt of the stretch, while the foreskin on either side, is just exercising its "springiness" (elastic limit).
